Plant Manager

Oversees daily plant operations, focusing on safety, efficiency, quality, staff management, and meeting production goals within budget.

Roles & Responsibilities

  1. Oversee Daily Operations: Manage all aspects of plant operations, ensuring smooth production and efficiency.
  2. Ensure Safety Compliance: Enforce safety standards and procedures to maintain a safe working environment.
  3. Optimize Production: Streamline processes to improve productivity, reduce costs, and meet production targets.
  4. Staff Management: Supervise, train, and motivate plant staff to enhance performance and foster teamwork.
  5. Budget & Resource Management: Monitor budget adherence, resource allocation, and cost control to achieve financial goals.

Sourcing Manager

Oversees supplier management, negotiations, and procurement to secure the best products, prices, and quality for kitchen appliances.

Roles & Responsibilities

  1. Supplier Management: Identify, evaluate, and maintain relationships with domestic and international suppliers for kitchen appliances.
  2. Negotiations: Secure the best prices, terms, and quality by negotiating contracts and terms with suppliers.
  3. Quality Assurance: Ensure all sourced products meet company standards for quality, design, and functionality.
  4. Market Analysis: Stay updated on industry trends, innovations, and competitor activities to make informed sourcing decisions.
  5. Cross-functional Collaboration: Work closely with the product development, manufacturing, and logistics teams to align sourcing strategies with business goals.

Industrial Design Lead

Responsible for designing concepts and specs that enhance product function, value, and appearance for users and manufacturers.

Roles & Responsibilities

  1. Concept Exploration & Research: Understand user needs and create captivating designs.
  2. Bringing Ideas to Life: Prototype and refine concepts, turning creative ideas into practical solutions.
  3. Innovative Prototyping: Use unconventional materials and technologies to craft functional and fascinating prototypes.
  4. Collaborative Endeavors: Work with engineers, marketers, and colleagues to align designs with company goals.
  5. User-Focused Design: Blend ergonomics and usability for intuitive products that enhance user experiences.

Design Engineer

The primary responsibility is to create and develop mechanical designs using various software tools according.

Roles & Responsibilities

  1. Design Creation: Responsible for conceptualizing, designing, and developing mechanical components, systems, and products using CAD (Computer-Aided Design) software such as SolidWorks, AutoCAD, CATIA, or similar tools.
  2. Client Interaction: Understanding and interpreting client requirements and specifications are crucial aspects of the role which often liaise with clients to gather information, clarify requirements, and ensure that designs meet their needs and expectations.
  3. Technical Expertise: Need a deep understanding of mechanical engineering principles, materials science, and manufacturing processes to create designs that are functional, reliable, and cost-effective.
  4. Documentation: Creating detailed technical drawings, specifications, and documentation to communicate design intent to manufacturing teams, suppliers, and other stakeholders. Accurate documentation is essential for ensuring that designs are translated into physical products correctly.

Quality Assurance Engineer

Ensuring product excellence by enforcing high standards and collaborating with production teams.

Roles & Responsibilities

  1. Quality Control Procedures:
    • Develop, implement, and maintain metal-specific quality control procedures.
    • Set quality standards for raw materials, components, and finished products.
    • Establish inspection criteria and testing methods to ensure adherence to standards.
  2. Inspection and Testing:
    • Regularly inspect metal materials and products for quality and accuracy.
    • Analyze test results and collaborate on corrective actions with production teams.
  3. Process Optimization:
    • Identify and implement process improvements for enhanced quality and efficiency.
    • Collaborate with engineers to minimize defects and optimize production.
  4. Documentation and Reporting:
    • Maintain detailed records of quality control activities and results.
    • Generate comprehensive reports on quality performance and areas for improvement.
  5. Supplier and Vendor Management:
    • Conduct supplier audits and maintain strong relationships to address quality issues.
  6. Regulatory Compliance:
    • Stay updated on industry regulations and ensure compliance.
    • Participate in audits to demonstrate adherence to quality standards.
